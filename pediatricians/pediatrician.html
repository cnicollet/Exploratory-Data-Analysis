Price applied by Pediatricians

In this section, we are going to analyze the pediatrician data set, and try to answer some Questions.

People visit pediatricians frequently when they become parents. Sometimes, they struggle to find the best doctor or to have an appointment. In France, the main thing to watch out for is whether or not your doctor is ‘conventionné’, and if so, which type of ‘convention’ he has, Sector 1 or Sector 2. So for basic health insurance reimbursement, there is a price guideline which all doctors who choose to be in ‘Secteur 1’ have to stand by. If a doctor is ‘Secteur 2’, he or she can choose to make the consultation more expensive. if a doctor is ‘non-conventionné’ (sometimes also called ‘Secteur 3’) he is entirely free to choose his own prices and free to ignore all government guidelines.

CONCLUSION:

From this small data set we can see that pediatricians applies different prices: 28 euros for sector1, and higher for other sectors. It would be more interesting to see the average extra cost per city, but we don’t have enough data to see the difference. Applying extra cost for patients (extra price) might be influenced by several factors: the area(prices applied in big cities are diffrent from the countryside), experience of physicians, speciality, number of physicians in a particular area, private or public, sector1, 2 or 3, total population around, their income, etc.
